    EUROPE AND CENTRAL ASIA LATIN AMERICA & CARRIBIAN EAST ASIA & PACIFIC ARAB STATES SOUTH ASIA SUB-SAHARAN AFRICA

    The second chart shows the trend of HDI index over time from 1990 - 2019 by Regions. As we can see, the general trend of the Human Development Index is increasing steadily, meaning the standard of living across the globe is better nowadays comparing to the 90s. However, you can observe interesting intersections on the graph. For example in 2006, EAST ASIA & PACIFIC's HDI index surpassed ARAB STATES's.

  • Can you find the correlations?

    Y-aixs vs. X-axis

    By changing X-axis and Y-axis parameters, we can observe that HDI index correlates to Life Expectency at Birth, Expected Years of Schooling, GNI per Capita etc. These are some of the variables used to calculate the HDI index. For example, countries with higher Expected Years of Schooling tend to have higher Human Development Index as well.
